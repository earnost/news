An Open Letter to Lady Columbia

By The Editor • July 11, 2026

Dearest Columbia,

It is with joy that I write to you concerning your 250th birthday. You have truly been a bastion of freedom and democracy for the world to see. While your legacy has been incredible, I am saddened that their are some who wish to tarnish your image.

Your great history began with free people defeating the Empire of Brittania. You used the greatest of the Europeans minds to fashion a republic that would stand as the home of free men for hundreds of years. Once divided on slavery, you then made all your people free. While today there are more slaves in the world than ever before, you declared that no man should own another man over 150 years ago. Many overlook the fact that you freed all men in your midst and resent you for this past suffering. I give you great respect for the freedom you have shown the world.

There are those in your midst now that wish to see your freedom thwarted. On your highest councils there are selfish men and women who desire dominations and power for themselves. They have woven a web of lies to decieve you and your people. They do not respect your laws, your principles, or your history. I urge you that you must purge the evil thoughts that have plagued your people. Return to your roots of freedom, do not let Marxism destroy this great nation. I believe that you must make swift return to your roots; Roman law, Greek philosophy, and Christian morality. I believe that the logic given to those followers of Jesus Christ shall far surpass the psuedo-intelligence of the selfish elite.

This cultural Marxism disguised as some form of democratic socialism goes against your core values. Those toxic systems teach that it's acceptable to steal from hard working people and redistribute to facilitate lifestyles of laziness and degeneracy. Remember the principles of your founding. Remember that all men are created equal by God and deserve equal treatment under the law.

There is still time for you to return to your roots of liberty, justice, and freedom for all. I urge you to take action. I fear that if you do not reform soon, much of the damage to this country may be beyond repair.

Respectfully,
The Eikon Editorial Desk

The Value of Local Journalism

By Staff Writer • July 8, 2026

In today's day, local news outlets have commonly acquired more trust than than large corporate networks. Local reporters have a more vested interest in their community and getting the facts out, rather than promoting a specific agenda. Local journalism also isn't gatekept by degrees or certifications, anyone can practice good journalism and reporting on issues that they are passionate about.

Good local reporting teaches truth and patience. Most stories do not offer quick answers, often times we are left wondering motives even after the story has concluded.

The Christ centered perspective so often goes overlooked when discussing the hot topics of today's news. So much confusion arrises when we see self-proclaimed Christ followers on corporate television stations calling for violence against others. And often Christians are left wondering on which issues they should act, with different voices online calling them in so many different directions.
